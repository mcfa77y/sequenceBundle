WHAT ARE SEQUENCE BUNDLES?

Sequence Bundles is a novel method for visualising Multiple Sequence Alignments (MSAs). It supports interactive exploration of sequence data, sequence comparison and discovery of new sequence motifs that would otherwise remain hidden.

HOW DOES IT WORK?

In Sequence Bundles every sequence from the MSA is represented as a continuous line. Its curved shape corresponds to the sequence of residues in a protein or DNA. Lines are stacked one on top of another to visualise the entire alignment.

WHO MADE SEQUENCE BUNDLES?

Sequence Bundles was designed by Science Practice and developed by Science Practice in collaboration with the Goldman group at the European Bioinformatics Institute (EMBL-EBI) in Hinxton, UK.

WHAT ARE THEY GOOD FOR?

Sequence Bundles provide a holistic overview of sequence conservation, correlations and motifs in genomic and proteomic data. Well suited for human perception, it allows for intuitive exploration of alignments, hypotheses generation and gathering of insight.
In our BMC article we discuss findings made through using the Sequence Bundles visualisation method.
